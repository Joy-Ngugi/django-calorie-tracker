The number of calories you should consume per day depends on several factors, including your age, sex, activity level, and body size.
The Dietary Guidelines for Americans 2020--2025 suggest that the following ranges are appropriate for weight maintenance:

  • Women: 1,600–2,400 calories
  • Men: 2,200–3,000 calories

However, the average person is generally estimated to consume:

  • Men: 2,500 kilocalories (kcals) per day
  • Women: 2,000 kcals per day

You can use the Mifflin-St Jeor equation to estimate your daily caloric expenditure:

  1. Calculate your resting metabolic rate (RMR) using your age, sex, height, and weight
  2. Determine your activity level
  3. Multiply your RMR by your activity level

You can also track your weight, waist measurement, or how your clothes fit to get an idea of whether you're eating the right amount.
It's more important to focus on the quality of your diet and where your energy comes from than the exact number of calories you consume.
